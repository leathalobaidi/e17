Step 1: Opening (30 seconds)

+
You say
"Hi, is this {{OWNER_NAME}}? My name's [Your Name] from E17Studios. I'm a local web designer based in Walthamstow. I noticed {{BUSINESS_NAME}} has fantastic reviews on Google — {{REVIEW_COUNT}} reviews at {{RATING}} stars — but doesn't have a website yet. I actually built a free preview of what one could look like for you. Do you have 5 minutes?"
They might say
"I'm busy right now" → "No worries at all! When's a better time to call back? I'll send you the preview link on WhatsApp in the meantime."
Tip
Lead with their review count. It's a genuine compliment and shows you did your research. Never start with "I'm selling websites."

Step 2: Discovery — SPIN Questions (2 minutes)

+
Situation
"How do most of your customers find you right now? Is it mainly word of mouth, walk-ins, or Google?"
Problem
"Have you ever had anyone tell you they couldn't find you online? Or tried to look you up and couldn't find a website?"
Implication
"When someone searches 'dry cleaners near me' and can't find your website, they usually just go to the first result that does have one. That's potentially {{LOST_CUSTOMERS}} customers a month going to competitors."
Need-Payoff
"If you had a professional website that showed up on Google with all your great reviews front and centre, how much extra business do you think that could bring in?"
Tip
Listen more than you talk. The goal is to get THEM to say they need a website, not to tell them they do.

Step 3: Demo — Show the Preview (2 minutes)

+
You say
"Let me send you the link right now — have a look on your phone while we chat. I've already set it up with your real Google reviews, your address, opening hours, and an embedded map."
Tip
Send the WhatsApp link while on the phone. Wait for their reaction. Their first "wow" or "oh nice" is your buying signal.
Walk through
"As you can see, it's mobile-friendly — most of your customers will find you on their phone. The click-to-call button means they can ring you with one tap. And the Google Map at the bottom helps people get directions straight away."
Common reaction
"This looks really good actually" → "Thank you! And this is just the preview — once activated, it'll be on your own domain with SSL security and SEO optimisation."

Step 4: Pricing (1 minute)

+
You say
"So the way it works is really simple. There's no upfront cost — I've already built it for you for free. If you want to keep it live, it's just £49 a month. That includes hosting, SSL, SEO, monthly reports, and any changes you need. No contract — cancel anytime."
Anchor
"Most freelancers would charge £2,000+ just to build something like this, plus £30/month hosting. With us, you get everything for less than £1.65 a day."
ROI Pitch
"If your average job is £35 and the website brings in just 2 extra customers a month, that's £70 in revenue from a £49 investment. It pays for itself with the first customer."

Step 5: Close (1 minute)

+
Assumptive close
"So shall I get this activated for you? I can have it live on your own domain by tomorrow."
Alternative close
"Would you prefer the £29 Starter plan or the £49 Professional with the review showcase and reports?"
If yes
"Brilliant! I'll send you a quick setup form on WhatsApp — takes 2 minutes to fill in. Your site will be live within 48 hours."
If not yet
"No problem at all. The preview stays live for 30 days. Have a think and I'll check in next week. No pressure whatsoever."
Tip
Always leave the door open. Many conversions happen on the 2nd or 3rd touchpoint, not the first call.

Objection Handlers

"It's too expensive"

+
Response
"I totally understand. Let me put it this way — £49 a month is about £1.65 a day. If your average job is £35, the website only needs to bring you ONE extra customer a month to pay for itself twice over. And with your {{REVIEW_COUNT}} reviews, you're already more trusted than competitors — you just need to be visible online."

"I need to think about it"

+
Response
"Absolutely, take your time. The preview stays live for 30 days so there's no rush. Can I ask — is there anything specific you're unsure about? Sometimes I can clear things up right now and save you the thinking."

"I don't need a website"

+
Response
"I hear you — and your business has clearly done well without one! But 97% of people search online before visiting a local business. Right now, when someone searches 'dry cleaners Walthamstow', your competitors with websites show up first. A website isn't about replacing what works — it's about catching the customers you're currently missing."

"My nephew can build one"

+
Response
"That's great that you have someone who can help! The thing is, building a site is the easy part — maintaining it, keeping it secure, optimising it for Google, and making sure it loads fast on mobile is the ongoing work. That's what the £49 covers. Plus if anything breaks or you need changes, you just message me."

"What if I want to cancel?"

+
Response
"You can cancel anytime — literally just send me a message. No notice period, no exit fees, no questions asked. If you cancel, your site goes offline but I'll export all your content and give it to you. Zero risk."
